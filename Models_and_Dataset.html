Models Used for Crop Disease Detection

For the purpose of crop disease detection, several machine learning models were used, including:

  • Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs): These are widely used for image classification and disease detection tasks, as they excel at recognizing patterns in images.
  • ResNet-50: A deep convolutional neural network known for its efficiency in handling large datasets and complex patterns.
  • MobileNet-V2: A lightweight convolutional neural network optimized for mobile and embedded devices, ensuring faster inference times with a smaller footprint.
  • Vision Transformers (ViT): Modern models based on attention mechanisms that handle image classification tasks with enhanced accuracy by analyzing the global context of the image.

Why These Models?

The choice of these models ensures a balance between accuracy, computational efficiency, and scalability. Convolutional Neural Networks like ResNet-50 are known for their robustness in handling large image datasets, while Vision Transformers represent cutting-edge research in image classification, offering improved accuracy on complex tasks. MobileNet-V2 is chosen for its lightweight nature, making it suitable for real-time, low-power applications such as on-field crop disease detection.

Data Set Used

The dataset used for this project is sourced from the open-source PlantVillage platform, which consists of over 50,000 images of healthy and infected plant leaves.

Data Set Description

The dataset contains images of leaves from different plants, with various diseases affecting each category. We used data from four specific crops: grapes, apples, corn, and tomatoes, which were classified into healthy and infected categories. The dataset is further divided into training and testing sets to evaluate the performance of the models.

Data Set Summary

  • Grapes: Healthy and infected grape leaves.
  • Apples: Healthy and infected apple leaves.
  • Corn: Healthy and infected corn leaves.
  • Tomatoes: Healthy and infected tomato leaves across 10 different categories.

The images are preprocessed by resizing them to a uniform dimension (224x224 pixels) and normalized to ensure consistency. Data augmentation techniques such as rotation and flipping were applied to enhance the robustness of the models.
